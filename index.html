SEO stands for search engine optimization and is the process of using certain methods to improve how well a website appears in search engine rankings. This is accomplished by keywords, keyword phrases, or on-site content that are targeted to match the needs of specific target audiences. SEO can be highly technical and involve very intricate strategies, but it's not at all difficult to learn.

Search engines, like Google, use spiders (also called crawlers) to scan websites for content and then categorize those sites for their searchable databases. SEO practices are designed to improve a web page's ranking on those databases so more people can find the content. While search engines do not share their exact techniques, we can effectively optimize our site just by following a few principles.

The key concept in SEO is keywords. The more specific the keyword used, the higher a website will rank on search engine results pages (SERPs). For example, if you were selling pepperoni pizza, you could use keywords such as "pizza," "pizza delivery," "pizza restaurants," etc. But if you tried to get ranked for a less specific keyword like "recipe," you would never be able to compete with all the recipe sites already out there.
